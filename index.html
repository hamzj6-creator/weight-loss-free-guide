The Ritual

Six checkpoints, spread across your day

This is written as a sequence on purpose — each one sets up the next. Skip a couple, still fine. Do all six most days, and the compounding is where the real change comes from.

01On waking

Rehydrate before you caffeinate

A glass of water first thing kickstarts digestion and often clears up the "hunger" that's actually mild dehydration. Have your coffee after, not instead of.

02Breakfast

Lead every meal with protein and fibre

Eggs, yoghurt, oats, lentils — whatever fits your kitchen. Protein and fibre are what actually keep you full for hours, which is what stops the 11am snack spiral.

03Midday

Move for 20–30 minutes, at any pace

A brisk walk counts. You don't need a gym membership to start — you need consistent daily movement your body can actually recover from.

043–4x / week

Add resistance training

Bodyweight squats, resistance bands, or weights — muscle is what keeps your metabolism higher even at rest. This is the step most people skip and most regret skipping.

05Evening

Eat without a screen in front of you

Distracted eating is the single easiest way to overeat without noticing. Ten device-free minutes per meal is enough to make a real difference.

06Before bed

Protect 7–9 hours of sleep

Poor sleep raises the hormones that drive hunger and cravings the next day. If you fix nothing else on this list, fixing sleep alone moves the needle.

Do I need to do all six habits from day one?

No. Pick two you're not doing today — usually water and sleep are the easiest wins — and build from there over a couple of weeks.
